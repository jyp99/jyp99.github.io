This agreement specifies the duties, rights and responsibilities, and any other necessary points between NHN Entertainment Corp. ("the Company") and Users in relation to the usage of the PAYCO ID Service, which enables access using a single ID to each "Individual Service" provided by the Company and its Affiliates.

1. Definitions

Terms used in this agreement shall be defined as follows.

  1. (1) "PAYCO ID Service" shall mean an "Individual Service" that can manage certification of User IDs and Passwords, change of User information, User registration, and User deletion.
  2. (2) "Individual Service" shall mean a Service offered by the Company or an Affiliate that can be accessed by Users regardless of the particular device (PC, TV, or wireless apparatus such as a mobile device) being used.
  3. (3) "User" shall mean a customer who accesses the PAYCO ID Service and receives an ID by acceding to a terms of use agreement with the Company according to this agreement.
  4. (4) "ID" shall mean an electronic mail address and/or mobile telephone number selected by a User and approved by the Company for User identification and Service use.
  5. (5) "Password" shall mean a combination of letters and/or numbers chosen by a User to be used as a password after the User has been issued an ID and confirmed agreement to membership.
  6. (6) "Affiliate" shall mean an entity that has concluded an affiliate agreement and is offering an "Individual Service" that can be accessed using an ID.
  7. (7) "PAYCO Points" shall mean an item provided by the Company to a User when that User uses or purchases an Individual Service, and may be used on an Individual Service with which the User is registered, and the particular details of the acquisition and usage of the same shall be as under Section 16 of this agreement.

2. Posting of and Amendments to This Agreement

  1. (1) So that the contents of this agreement are easily accessible by Users, the Company will provide it by means of publication on the "Individual Services" and related screens.
  2. (2) The Company may amend this agreement in accordance with related laws including the Regulation of Standardized Contracts Act and the Law Regarding the Promotion of Information and Communication Network Use and Protection of Information.
  3. (3) When the Company does amend this agreement, the effective date, substance, and purposes of the amendments will be specified and, at least seven days (30 days in case of amendments that disfavor or are important to Users) before the effective date and until a reasonable time following the effective date, will be published on "Individual Service" screens and related screens.
  4. (4) When the Company amends this agreement, the assent of a User regarding the application of the amended Agreement will be ascertained after the publication of the amended Agreement. However, when the amended Agreement is published, if a User does not express either assent or refusal, then it shall be published as if the User had assented and in such case, if the User fails to express refusal by the effective date of the Agreement, then the User shall be treated as having assented to the amended Agreement.
  5. (5) If the User does not assent to the amended agreement, the Company will be unable to apply the amended agreement to that User and in such case the User and the Company shall be able to terminate the terms of use agreement.

3. Interpretation of This Agreement

  1. (1) The Company may set up separate terms of use agreements and policies (hereinafter "Individual Service Agreements Etc.") for Individual Services, and when such agreements conflict with this agreement the Individual Service Agreements Etc. shall take priority.
  2. (2) Details and interpretations not defined in this agreement shall accord with applicable laws including the Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commercial Transactions, the Regulation of Standardized Contracts Act, the Law Regarding the Promotion of Information and Communication Network Use and Protection of Information, and the Cultural Industry Basic Promotion Act.

4. Applications for Use and Methods

  1. (1) An individual wishing to use the PAYCO ID Service must apply by assenting to this agreement and providing all requested information to the Company.
  2. (2) Users must disclose true information regarding themselves when applying for Service under clause (1) above. Any User who provides false information or unlawfully uses the information of another person shall be unable to assert rights of membership under this agreement, and the Company shall be able to cancel or revoke the terms of use agreement without regard to any separate compensation.

5. Acceptance of and Limitations to Applications for Use

  1. (1) The Company shall approve applications for use, without particular reasons, when a User applies by providing true information in response to the company's requests for information. However, the Company is unable to approve applications falling into any of the following categories:
    1. ① Where the application for use violates Section 4
    2. ② Where approval procedures determined by the company, such as electronic mail approval, are not completed
    3. ③ Where an individual who has previously lost membership credentials is unable to obtain the Company's approval for a membership reapplication
    4. ④ Where an application for use is made with the goal of violating the Law Regarding the Promotion of Information and Communication Network Use and Protection of Information and related statutes
    5. ⑤ Where an application for use comes from a child 14 years of age or less
    6. ⑥ Where it is determined that approval would be inappropriate for other reasons based on 1 through 4
  2. (2) Details and interpretations not defined in this agreement shall accord with applicable laws including the Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commercial Transactions, the Regulation of Standardized Contracts Act, the Law Regarding the Promotion of Information and Communication Network Use and Protection of Information, and the Cultural Industry Basic Promotion Act.
  3. (3) Where the Company denies or postpones approval of a membership registration under clauses (1) or (2) above, the Company generally will notify the applicant of the same.
  4. (4) The time of establishment of a terms of use agreement under this agreement shall be the time at which the Company expresses that User registration is complete according to the application process.

6. Provision of and Amendments to User Information

  1. (1) Where a User must provide information to the Company under this agreement, truthful information shall be provided, and no compensation shall be provided to a User if damages are sustained due to the provision of false information.
  2. (2) The Company may request additional personal information, through an Individual Service and with the assent of a User, and such personal information properly provided thereby may be accessed and changed by said User at any time through a personal information management screen. However, it shall not be possible to change the true name, date of birth, gender, ID, etc. needed for the Individual Service.
  3. (3) When information provided during User registration changes, a User must notify the Company of the changed information either online or via electronic mail or some other means.
  4. (4) The Company shall not be liable for damages resulting from any changes to information not being provided to the Company as outlined in clause (3) above.

7. Protection and Management of Personal Information

  1. (1) The Company will strive to protect the personal information of Users in accordance with the Law Regarding the Promotion of Information and Communication Network Use and Protection of Information. Applicable laws and the Company's personal information collection policies will apply to the protection and usage of Users' personal information.
  2. (2) The Company's personal information collection policies shall not apply on third-party websites or Services that are simply linked from the Company's public website.
  3. (3) The Company shall not bear sole responsibility regarding all information, including account information of Users, which is exposed due to acts of a User.

8. Obligations Regarding Management of Users' IDs and Passwords

  1. (1) Users shall be responsible for managing their ID and Password, and shall not allow any third party to use them. A User shall be liable for any damages incurred due to the User's negligent management or allowing the use of the User's ID and Password by a third party.
  2. (2) If a User's ID and Password are stolen, or if a User is aware of third party use of the User's ID and Password, the User shall immediately notify the Company and comply with the Company's guidance.
  3. (3) With regards to clause (2) above, if the User fails to notify such incident to the Company, or if the User fails to comply with the Company's guidance even though the User notified, the Company will not be liable for any damages incurred.
  4. (4) Users shall change their Passwords regularly, and the Company will allow Users to change their Passwords any time they desire.

9. Notifications to Users

  1. (1) When the Company provides a notification to any Users, the Company may use emails or electronic memos as designated by the Users, unless there is a separate provision in this agreement.
  2. (2) If the notification is for all Users, the Company may post on the Company's bulletin board for more than 7 days as a substitute for the notification described in clause (1) above.

10. Obligations of the Company

  1. (1) The Company shall not conduct any actions this agreement prohibits or commit any offenses against public morals, and shall do its best to provide continuous and stable Services.
  2. (2) So that all Users can use its Services securely, the Company shall maintain security system(s) to protect private information (including credit information), and shall announce and abide by personal information collection policies. The Company shall not reveal or provide a User's personal information to a third party, except for purposes as provided in this agreement and the personal information collection policies.
  3. (3) The Company shall process suggestions or complaints raised by Users with regards to their use of its Services when such suggestions or complaints are deemed reasonable. For such suggestions or complaints raised by Users, the Company shall use its bulletin board or emails to communicate to the Users about the handling process and results.
  4. (4) If equipment or facilities become impeded or data is lost in the course of the Company improving its Services in an attempt to provide continuous and stable Service, the Company shall do its best to repair or restore such without delay, unless there are inevitable circumstances such as natural disasters, emergencies, or defects and failures that cannot be resolved with the current technology.

11. Obligations of Users

  1. (1) Users shall not take any of the following actions.
    1. ① Registration of false information when applying for any Service or changing information
    2. ② Unlawful use of others' personal information
    3. ③ Falsely representing oneself to be employees, managers, or other persons affiliated with the Company
    4. ④ Changes to the information provided by the Company
    5. ⑤ Transmitting or posting information the Company has prohibited (including computer programs)
    6. ⑥ Making, distributing, using, or promoting computer programs, equipment, or devices the Company did not provide or authorize
    7. ⑦ Infringement of intellectual property including the Company's and other third parties' copyrights
    8. ⑧ Defaming or obstructing business of the Company and other third parties
    9. ⑨ Making public or posting to the Company obscene or violent messages, images, sounds, and other information against public morals
    10. ⑩ Using the Company's Services for the purposes of profit, sales, promotion, or political activities without consent from the Company
    11. ⑪ Other actions that are prohibited by related laws, or actions that are not allowed under public morals and social norms, or other wrongful actions
  2. (2) Users shall confirm and respect applicable laws, the terms of this agreement, rules related to usage information and Services, and matters communicated by the Company, and shall not engage in activities that disturb other companies' operations.

12. Provision of Services, Etc.

  1. (1) The Company shall provide the following Services to Users.
    1. ① Authentication services that can be accessed with a single ID and Password from an Individual Service
    2. ② Individually customized product recommendation Service through analysis of User trends, User interests, usage records, etc.
    3. ③ Other services provided by ocompany
  2. (2) Generally, Service(s) will be offered with no interruptions for 24 hours per day without exception throughout the year. However, the Company may restrict an Individual Service and the usage time and/or number of uses available to a User according to Company policy and applicable law.
  3. (3) The Company may temporarily interrupt the provision of Service(s) for the maintenance of information communications facilities and/or equipment including repair inspections, substitutions and breakdowns, communications interruptions, and other substantial operational reasons.
  4. (4) In cases falling under clause (2) or clause (3) above, the Company shall notify the User under Section 9. However, in cases where the Company is unable to make prior notification due to unavoidable reasons, the Company may make notification afterwards.
  5. (5) When necessary for the provision of its Services, the Company may conduct routine inspections, and the time of routine inspections will be communicated through a Service provision screen.

13. Changes to Services

  1. (1) When there are substantial reasons and in accordance with operational and/or technical needs, the Company may alter either a portion or all of the Services it provides.
  2. (2) When there are changes in the substance, methods of use, or times of use of a Service, matters such as the reasons for the change, the substance of the change in the Service, and the effective date shall be disclosed in the Service's initial screen prior to the change.
  3. (3) The Company may in whole or in part alter, suspend, or change Services provided for free when necessary under Company policy and operations, and no compensation will be made to Users without specific provision of applicable law.

14. Provision of Information and Placement of Advertising

  1. (1) When determined by the Company to be necessary and while a User is using a Service, information including commercial information and replies to customer inquiries may be provided to the User by means of announcements, electronic mail, and SMS. However, the User may at any time refuse to receive the same through electronic mail, etc.
  2. (2) The Company may provide advertisements to Users in accordance with the Law Regarding the Promotion of Information and Communication Network Use and Protection of Information and appropriate procedures.

15. Reversion of Rights

  1. (1) Rights of copyright and intellectual property relating to the PAYCO ID Service shall remain with the Company.
  2. (2) The Company will, with regard to the PAYCO ID Service, grant to Users the right to use the Service according to conditions decided by the Company, and Users shall not be able to assign, trade, or otherwise dispose of the same.

16. Cyber Points

  1. (1) Methods for acquiring and using PAYCO Points shall be in accordance with Individual Service Agreements Etc.
  2. (2) PAYCO Points shall automatically expire after 24 months from the time of initial acquisition, and when PAYCO Points expire following their validity period, the Company shall notify Users in advance of which PAYCO Points are to expire, the expiration time, and other matters related to PAYCO Points' expiration by means of the electronic mail selected by the Users or by electronic memo. However, according to the type and nature of the PAYCO Points, the expiration date of specific PAYCO Points may be differently determined, and in such cases the Company shall separately notify Users of any such terms.
  3. (3) Where an error occurs in the acquisition of PAYCO Points, an application to correct the same may be made within a reasonable time, and the Company may, where necessary, pursue corrective measures in PAYCO Points after proceeding with a confirmation process and depending on the results of said process.

17. Termination of Membership

  1. (1) Users may at any time apply for revocation of a terms of use agreement (cancellation of membership) through an information management menu or customer service on the Service screen. Where a User applies for cancellation of membership, the Company may confirm that the applicant is the User in question, and will make an immediate decision in accordance with applicable law. However, the Company may restrict immediate cancellations for reasons such as to prevent the misuse of a Service during a certain period of time following membership registration by a User.
  2. (2) Notwithstanding the previous clause, the Company may restrict immediate cancellations for reasons such as provisions of an Individual Service Agreements Etc. or prevention of misuse of a Service by a User.
  3. (3) Where the Company revokes a PAYCO ID Service terms of use agreement, any terms of use agreement of a Service provided by the Company or an Affiliate shall be simultaneously revoked, and stored PAYCO Points shall immediately expire.
  4. (4) Where a User revokes a PAYCO ID Service terms of use agreement, the Company will, except in cases where personal information of the User is retained, destroy all data including account information of the User immediately upon revocation and in accordance with applicable law and the Company's personal information management policy.

18. Usage Restrictions

  1. (1) Where a User violates his or her obligations under this agreement or disturbs normal operations, thereby causing harm to the Company intentionally or negligently in violation of existing law, the Company may restrict the ID's login and/or revoke the terms of use agreement.
  2. (2) Where the Company revokes a terms of use agreement, the User will be notified under Section 9 of the reasons for and date of the revocation.
  3. (3) Where there is reason for the Company to restrict usage, the Company will not compensate Users for damages incurred thereby.
  4. (4) Users may, by procedure determined by the Company, make formal objections to a restriction on use imposed under this Section. At such time, and where the Company determines the objection to be proper, the Company will immediately re-open the use of Service.
  5. (5) The Company, in accordance with the Law Regarding the Promotion of Information and Communication Network Use and Protection of Information and its implementing regulations, and to protect the personal information of a User who has not used a Service during a one-year period, may take necessary steps such as destruction of said personal information. Further, where there are objective concerns over harm from use of account information or where a User has not logged in during a period of one year or longer, and for the effective protection of the personal information of the User and of business operations, the Company may take necessary steps including interim measures, restriction of use, and deletion of account information. Where steps are taken under this clause, the Company will notify the User under Section 9 of the fact, date, and clauses of the necessary steps taken, which may include destruction of account information, up to 30 days before the date of such steps.

19. Limitation of Liability

  1. (1) In cases where the Company is unable to provide Service(s) due to force majeure such as a natural disaster, the obligation to provide Service(s) shall be suspended.
  2. (2) The Company shall bear no liability for interruptions in Service(s), difficulties in use, or revocation of this agreement caused by the actions of a User.
  3. (3) The Company shall bear no liability for matters related to the information, materials, actual reliability, accuracy, etc. of or provided by a User related to the Service(s).
  4. (4) The Company shall not be liable for cases of disputes between Users or between Users and third parties that arise through the Services.
  5. (5) The Company shall bear no liability regarding the use of Services that are provided for free without specific provision of applicable law.

20. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

  1. (1) Litigation between the Company and Users shall be governed by the law of the Republic of Korea.
  2. (2) Jurisdiction over disputes that arise between the Company and Users shall be decided under the terms of the Civil Procedure Code.
